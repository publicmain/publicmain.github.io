FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our school, admissions, courses, and student life.

Admissions & Application

Application deadlines vary by course. Generally, applications for the academic year starting in January should be submitted by October of the previous year. For mid-year intakes, please check our calendar or contact our admissions office.
Entry requirements vary by course level. For SEC courses, students need to complete secondary education. For O-Level, A-Level, and IAL courses, specific academic qualifications and English proficiency are required. Please refer to our detailed entry requirements page.
International students need to submit additional documents including passport copies, visa documentation, and certified translations of academic records. We recommend applying through our authorized agents in your country for assistance with visa applications.

Courses & Programs

O-Level is typically a 2-year program for students aged 14-16, covering fundamental subjects. A-Level is a more advanced 2-year program for students aged 16-18, preparing them for university entrance. Both are internationally recognized qualifications.
We offer a wide range of subjects including Mathematics, English, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Economics, Business Studies, and more. Subject availability may vary by course level and student demand.
Course duration varies: SEC courses are typically 1-2 years, O-Level is 2 years, A-Level is 2 years, and IAL is flexible with modular examinations. Please check individual course pages for specific duration details.

Fees & Payments

School fees include tuition, examination fees, student materials, and access to school facilities. Additional costs may include textbooks, uniform (if applicable), and optional activities. Please refer to our detailed fee structure for specific inclusions.
Yes, we offer various scholarships and financial aid programs based on academic merit and financial need. Applications for scholarships should be submitted along with your admission application. Please contact our admissions office for more details.
We accept various payment methods including bank transfers, credit cards, and installment plans. Payment schedules and methods are detailed in our fee structure document. International students may have different payment options available.

Student Life & Support

We provide comprehensive student support including academic counseling, career guidance, psychological support, and assistance with university applications. Our student support team is available to help with both academic and personal challenges.
Yes, we offer a variety of CCA (Co-Curricular Activities) including sports, arts, debate, robotics, and community service. These activities help students develop leadership skills and pursue their interests beyond academics.
Regular school hours are from 8:30 AM to 3:30 PM, Monday to Friday. Some classes and activities may extend beyond these hours. Students are expected to arrive on time and attend all scheduled classes.
