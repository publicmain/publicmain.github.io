Policies & Procedures

Important policies for students and guardians

Course Transfer, Withdrawal & Deferral Policy
Key Points
  • Processing Time: ≤4 weeks (including internal appeals)
  • Refund Processing: Within 7 working days after approval
  • Deferral Period: Maximum 6 months (subject to approval)
  • Transfer Conditions: Subject to course availability and eligibility
Application Process
  1. Submit written request to Student Services
  2. Include supporting documents (if applicable)
  3. Await review and decision (up to 4 weeks)
  4. Receive written notification of outcome

Note: All requests must be submitted in writing. Verbal requests will not be processed. Refunds are subject to the refund policy outlined in the student contract.

Dispute Resolution Policy
Resolution Timeline
  • Informal Resolution: Within 3 working days
  • Formal Complaint: Within 10 working days
  • Internal Appeal: Within 21 working days
  • External Mediation: Referred to CPE/ACRA if unresolved
How to File a Complaint
  1. Step 1: Discuss with relevant staff/teacher informally
  2. Step 2: Submit formal written complaint to Student Services
  3. Step 3: Complaint reviewed by management committee
  4. Step 4: Receive written response with resolution
  5. Step 5: If unsatisfied, file internal appeal
  6. Step 6: External mediation (CPE/ACRA) if still unresolved

Contact: For complaints or disputes, email complaints@esic.edu.sg or visit Student Services office.

Attendance & Academic Progress Policy
Attendance Requirements
  • Minimum Attendance: 90% required for all courses
  • Daily Presence: Average 5 hours per day in school
  • Absence Limit: Cannot be absent for 7 consecutive days without authorization
  • Medical Leave: Medical certificate required for absences due to illness
Academic Progress Monitoring
  • Regular assessments and progress reports
  • Parent/guardian notifications for poor performance
  • Academic intervention plans for struggling students
  • Counseling and support services available

Warning: Failure to meet attendance requirements may result in Student Pass cancellation for international students and course termination.

Student Conduct Policy
Expected Behavior
  • Respect for staff, fellow students, and school property
  • Punctuality and regular attendance
  • Academic integrity (no plagiarism or cheating)
  • Appropriate dress code and personal presentation
  • Compliance with Singapore laws and regulations
Disciplinary Actions

Violations may result in:

  • Minor Offenses: Verbal warning, written warning
  • Moderate Offenses: Suspension, community service
  • Serious Offenses: Expulsion, police report
